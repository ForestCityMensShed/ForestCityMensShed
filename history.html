Men's Shed Movement and History

Origins and History

  • Early 1990s, Australia: Informal community initiatives began to address men's social isolation.
  • 1998: First recognized Men's Shed established in Tongala, Victoria by nurse Maxine Chaseling.
  • 2007: Formation of the Australian Men’s Shed Association (AMSA).
  • 2008 onward: International expansion to New Zealand, UK, Ireland, Canada, US, and beyond.

Purpose and Impact

  • Combat social isolation by providing a community space.
  • Support mental health through informal peer support and connection.
  • Health promotion via workshops and talks tailored to men’s health.
  • Skill-sharing and meaningful activity through hands-on projects.

Global Reach

  • Australia: Over 1,000 sheds supported by government funding.
  • United Kingdom: Hundreds of sheds with support from the UK Men’s Sheds Association.
  • Ireland: Rapid growth supported by local communities.
  • Canada and US: Expanding presence with national associations forming.

Key Values

  • Inclusivity: Open to all who are interested.
  • Community engagement: Focused on local improvement and service.
  • Non-competitive environment: Emphasis on participation over performance.
